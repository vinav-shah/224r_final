GHZ State (3 qubits) — PPO

GHZ is the most revealing circuit. PPO finds the correct 3-gate sequence (H → CX(0,1) → CX(1,2)) repeatedly — peaks of 0.86 (combined_weak) and 0.85 (combined_med) confirm the agent has learned the right strategy. The problem is catastrophic forgetting: because PPO is on-policy and discards past experience after each update, the fixed entropy coefficient periodically forces re-exploration, knocking the policy off the narrow peak. Whether the run ends in a peak or trough is essentially random — combined_med ended near a peak (0.49), combined_weak ended in a trough (0.20).

Learning curves — GHZ fidelity over training (400k steps)

Violent oscillation between ~0.13 (random) and 0.86 (optimal) is the signature PPO entropy-collapse cycle. Peaks increase over time but final fidelity is non-deterministic.

Peak vs final fidelity gap — evidence of catastrophic forgetting

The large peak–final gap is unique to GHZ. Bell, SWAP, and QFT do not show this pattern. It is the direct consequence of on-policy forgetting in a hard-exploration, sparse-reward environment.

Key finding: PPO can solve GHZ (peak ≥ 0.71 under all noise levels) but cannot reliably retain the solution. The final model checkpoint may be captured at a trough. This motivates the SAC comparison.

SWAP (2 qubits) — PPO

All four SWAP runs converged to depth 0 — the agent learned to issue STOP immediately. This corresponds to ~0.49 fidelity, the identity circuit fidelity for the SWAP target. The standard 3-CNOT decomposition accumulates noise faster than it improves overlap with the target: at p₂ = 2–10% per CNOT, three sequential CNOTs lose 6–30% fidelity. Qiskit's transpiler produces the same depth-0 result under these noise models. This is a meaningful negative result: SWAP is uncompilable under these noise levels with this gate set.

Final fidelity by noise level — SWAP converges to identity

~0.49 is the noise-floor fidelity of |00⟩ vs the SWAP target. The agent reaches this within ~4k steps and stays there.

Quantum Fourier Transform (2 qubits) — PPO

QFT is a partial success. PPO reaches ~0.42 fidelity under weak/medium noise vs a random baseline of ~0.25. The theoretical ceiling (Qiskit) is ~0.85–0.90, so the agent reaches roughly half the achievable gap. The limit is structural: the discrete angle set (Rx/Ry/Rz at π/4, π/2, π) cannot exactly represent the controlled-phase rotations QFT requires. The agent finds approximate decompositions but is bounded by gate-set expressivity, not exploration failure. Note that QFT does not show oscillation — it finds a stable approximate solution and holds it.

Discrete SAC on GHZ — Early Training (≤16% of budget)

SAC runs are in progress — 51k–64k of 400k steps complete. A sign bug in the alpha loss was discovered and fixed (alpha_loss = -(log_alpha * (entropy - target)) → alpha_loss = log_alpha * (entropy - target).detach()). All data below is from the corrected runs launched after the fix.

The temperature parameter α behaves correctly after the fix: it decays from 1.0 toward ~0.001 as entropy stabilizes near the target (2.37 nats). Around step 43–47k all three combined-noise runs experience a sharp fidelity jump from ~0.13 to ~0.23–0.25 and hold that value stably — no crashing. This contrasts with PPO which oscillates violently at the same step count.
